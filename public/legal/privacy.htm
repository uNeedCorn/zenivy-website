3. Inboxless Service

Inboxless is a desktop email analysis and inbox cleanup tool that helps you understand your inbox and remove unwanted emails. This section explains how Inboxless accesses and processes your Gmail data.

3.1 Data We Access

When you connect your Gmail account to Inboxless, we request the gmail.modify permission, which allows us to:

Data Type Purpose
Email headers (sender, subject, date) Analyze sender statistics and categorize emails
Email content preview Identify newsletters, notifications, and email types
Email labels and status Understand read/unread status and categorization
List-Unsubscribe headers Provide one-click unsubscribe functionality

What We Do NOT Access

  • Email attachments or their contents
  • Your Google Contacts
  • Your Google Drive files
  • Any data from other Google services

3.2 Why We Need gmail.modify Permission

The gmail.modify scope enables Inboxless to:

  1. Read emails: Analyze your inbox and generate sender statistics
  2. Delete emails: Remove emails you select for cleanup (batch delete)
  3. Modify labels: Move emails to trash or archive when requested
Important: Inboxless only performs delete or modify actions when you explicitly initiate them. We never automatically delete or modify your emails.

3.3 How We Process Your Data

Inboxless is designed with privacy-first principles:

Principle Implementation
Local Processing All email analysis is performed on your device, not on our servers
No Upload Your email content is never transmitted to or stored on our servers
Immediate Disposal Analysis data is cleared from memory when you close the application
No Database We do not store your emails or analysis results

Technical Details

  • Email data is fetched directly from Gmail API to your device
  • All processing occurs in your computer's memory
  • OAuth tokens are securely stored in your operating system's keychain (macOS Keychain)
  • Our OAuth server only handles the authorization flow and never accesses your email content

3.4 Data Retention

Data Type Retention Period
Email analysis results Deleted immediately when you close the app
OAuth access tokens Stored in OS Keychain until you disconnect
User preferences Stored locally until you uninstall the app

We do NOT retain:

  • Your email content
  • Your email analysis history
  • Records of which emails you deleted

3.5 Data Sharing

We do NOT share your Gmail data with any third parties.

Inboxless does not include:

  • Advertising or tracking software
  • Analytics services that collect email data
  • Any mechanism to sell or share your data

3.6 How to Revoke Access

You can revoke Inboxless's access to your Gmail at any time:

Method 1: In Inboxless

  1. Open Inboxless
  2. Go to Settings
  3. Click "Disconnect Gmail"

Method 2: In Google Account

  1. Go to Google Account Security Settings
  2. Find "Inboxless" in the list
  3. Click "Remove Access"

After revoking access, Inboxless will no longer be able to access your Gmail, and locally stored tokens will be deleted.

5. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Encrypted Transmission: All data is transmitted over HTTPS
  • Secure Token Storage: OAuth tokens are stored in your OS keychain, not in plain text
  • No Server Storage: Email data never leaves your device
  • Minimal Data Collection: We only collect what's necessary for the service to function
